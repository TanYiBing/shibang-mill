Limestone

Introduction

The main component of limestone is calcium carbonate (CaCO3), a compound that is a common substance on the earth and can be found in rocks. Lime and limestone are used extensively as building materials and are important raw materials for many industries. Limestone can be directly processed into stone and fired into quicklime. Lime is divided into quicklime and slaked lime. The main component of quicklime is CaO, which is generally blocky, pure white, and pale gray or yellowish when containing impurities. The quicklime absorbs moisture or adds water to become slaked lime. The main component is Ca(OH)2
